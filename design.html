    Trillium Word Blending


    This was a design completed for my first graphic design course. You can see my first sketches on the left, as well as the finished product on the right. The goal was to impart more of a peaceful tone from the lighter tones.


    This project helped me understand how important readability is in text. The wrong font can render the communication of effective and relatable design ineffective.

    Typographic Mushrooms

    This design reflects my love of nature and my interest in foraging. I grew up exploring all the corners of Michigan with my family, so this design takes all of the mushrooms that I have seen across my home state into one design.


    One lesson that I took from this personal project was the use of complimentary fonts and effective cropping. Believe me when I say that it is hard to crop around biological sketches from an 18th-century lab manual!


    Weather App Mock-Up

    This was a design completed as a challenge for my first graphic design course. It was the first real combination of different elements, from typography and icons to complimentary colors and masking. I also wanted to make this application mock-up as interactive as possible; my hope was that the car would be pulled horizontally to change the time, as indicated by the screens if read from left to right. While I would by no means say that the addition of the car was "bold," I definitely learned the value of taking risks in design and understanding their implications to users.
